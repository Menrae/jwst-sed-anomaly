A Search for Anomalous Spectral Energy Distributions in High-Redshift Galaxies with JWST: A Data-Driven Approach

Motivation & Background
  • JWST/NIRCam photometric surveys (CEERS, JADES) now provide catalogs large and deep enough to search systematically for galaxies whose SEDs deviate from standard population-synthesis expectations.
  • Unsupervised anomaly detection has precedent in astronomy—outlier searches in SDSS spectra and quasar catalogs—but has seen little systematic application to JWST-era photometric residuals.
  • Even a well-characterized null result constitutes a quantitative, galactic-scale upper limit on non-standard astrophysical processes—the deliberately conservative framing motivating this analysis.

Discussion & Conclusions
  • A reproducible, ensemble anomaly pipeline (Isolation Forest + UMAP/DBSCAN) flags a well-defined SED-outlier population directly from public JWST photometric catalogs, with no survey-specific tuning required.
  • Cross-matching flagged sources against an AGN reference catalog (Milliquas) and an emission-line-contamination criterion explains the majority of anomalies as known astrophysical processes, not novel physics.
  • The residual “unexplained” population, once fully characterized, sets a conservative, quantitative upper limit on non-standard interpretations rather than constituting a detection claim.
  • Next steps: extend the pipeline to COSMOS-Web, and pursue spectroscopic follow-up of the highest-confidence unexplained outliers.
